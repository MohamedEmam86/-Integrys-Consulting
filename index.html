Six-Step Materiality Process

1
Sector Scan
Identify sector-relevant ESG topics using GRI, SASB, and Egyptian regulatory frameworks. Benchmark against peer companies.
2
Stakeholder Survey
Structured surveys and interviews with investors, customers, employees, regulators, and community. Score importance (1–5).
3
Business Impact Analysis
Score each topic for financial impact, regulatory exposure, and reputational risk using a double materiality lens.
4
Materiality Matrix
Plot topics on a 2×2 matrix (stakeholder importance × business impact). Identify top 10–15 material topics.
5
Board Validation
Board and management workshop to validate material topics, align with strategy and risk register, and secure sign-off.
6
Disclosure & Target-Setting
Embed material topics in ESG report. Set KPIs and targets. Annual review cycle. Link to executive remuneration where applicable.

FRA · MANDATORY
FRA Resolutions 107 & 108 of 2021 — what mandatory ESG/TCFD disclosure means for you
▾
The FRA's board issued Resolutions 107 and 108 in July 2021, requiring: (1) all EGX-listed companies, and (2) FRA-regulated non-bank financial companies with paid-up capital or net equity of EGP 100 million or more, to publish annual ESG disclosures. Companies at or above EGP 500 million must additionally publish TCFD-aligned climate disclosures. First disclosures covered FY2022 and were filed with 2023 annual statements — this is now a recurring annual obligation, not a one-time exercise.
CBE · BANKS & NBFIs
CBE Guiding Principles (2021) vs Binding Regulations (Nov 2022) — know the difference
▾
In July 2021 the CBE issued voluntary Guiding Principles for Sustainable Finance (6 principles: capacity-building, sustainable finance uptake, stakeholder engagement, climate risk management, sustainable internal operations, and reporting). In November 2022 the CBE converted this into Binding Regulations — every bank must now establish a dedicated sustainability/sustainable-finance department, embed ESG risk into credit and investment policies, and report to the CBE. If your bank or NBFI only has the 2021 principles on file without the 2022 binding structure in place, you are behind the current requirement.
PDPL · URGENT — 2026 DEADLINE
Data Protection Law 151/2020: the 2025 Executive Regulations changed your deadline
▾
Egypt's Personal Data Protection Law (151/2020) sat without operative Executive Regulations for years. That changed on 1 November 2025, when Prime Ministerial Decree 816/2025 issued the Executive Regulations — introducing a graduated licensing/permit regime for data processing, mandatory breach notification (72 hours to the Personal Data Protection Center, 3 working days to affected individuals), and full data-subject rights (access, correction, objection, withdrawal of consent). Organisations have a one-year grace period, meaning full enforcement lands around October 2026. If you process customer or employee data — which every PSP, bank and consulting firm does — this is a live, time-boxed compliance project now, not a future one.

LABOR LAW · HR/GRC IMPACT
Labor Law 14/2025 — what changed for your HR and governance policies
▾
Law 14/2025 took effect 1 September 2025, fully replacing Law 12/2003 and Law 125/2010 — Egypt's most significant labour reform in decades (298 articles). For GRC purposes, the key impacts are: written Arabic employment contracts in four copies (employer, employee, social insurance office, labour authority); maternity leave extended from 3 to 4 months, usable up to three times; a new training-fund contribution (0.25% of minimum insured salary per employee); and specialised labour courts operating from October 2025 for faster dispute resolution. Every HR policy, employment contract template and disciplinary procedure in your policy suite should be re-checked against Law 14/2025 — this is a Social-pillar (S) item under our I-ESG model as well as a core GRC policy update.
FATF · AML/CFT
Egypt's 2025 FATF Mutual Evaluation — what it means for your AML programme
▾
Egypt is not on the FATF grey or black list. Its 2025 Mutual Evaluation Report rated Egypt Compliant or Largely Compliant on 37 of the 40 FATF Recommendations, with 4 areas still only Substantially Effective on the effectiveness scale. Practically, this means regulators (CBE, FRA, EMLCU) are under active pressure to tighten supervision, STR quality and beneficial-ownership transparency — so expect more inspections, more documentation requests, and less tolerance for template-only AML policies that are not actually followed. A programme that looks good on paper but lacks evidenced execution (training logs, STR trails, risk-rating records) is now a real regulatory exposure, not just a best-practice gap.
AML CONCEPTS
KYC vs CDD vs EDD — the practical difference at onboarding
▾
KYC (Know Your Customer) is identity verification: who is this person or entity, really? CDD (Customer Due Diligence) goes further — understanding the customer's business, expected transaction behaviour, and source of funds, so unusual activity can be spotted later. EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence) applies to higher-risk customers — politically exposed persons (PEPs), cash-intensive businesses, customers from higher-risk jurisdictions — and requires deeper verification, senior sign-off, and more frequent monitoring. Most Egyptian PSPs and DNFBPs get flagged in inspections not because KYC is missing, but because CDD/EDD is not risk-tiered — everyone gets the same shallow check regardless of actual risk.
ESG STANDARDS
IFRS S1/S2 vs GRI vs the EGX template — which one applies to you?
▾
GRI Standards are impact-focused — they report your effect on the economy, environment and people (double materiality's 'impact' side) and are the base layer expected under the FRA/EGX template. IFRS S1/S2 are investor-focused — they report sustainability and climate risks that could affect your company's own financial value ('financial materiality'), and are mandatory for larger FRA-regulated entities (≥EGP 500m) alongside TCFD. The EGX ESG template is Egypt's own structured disclosure form, built to reference GRI indicators and, increasingly, TCFD/IFRS S2 climate metrics. In practice, most EGX-listed clients need all three working together: GRI for the narrative and impact indicators, IFRS S2 for climate financial risk, and the EGX template as the delivery format regulators expect.
